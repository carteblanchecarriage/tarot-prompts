The 4 Art Styles

Royal Court

Classic

Traditional tarot card art with ornate gold borders, vintage 1900s illustration style, deep navy and purple color palette with gold accents. Best for: Classic Rider-Waite inspired decks.

Dream Walker

Pop Surrealism

Low brow pop surrealism with vibrant neon colors, thick black outlines, warped whimsical characters, and dreamlike absurd scenes. Best for: Modern, edgy collector's editions.

80s retro surrealism with vaporwave colors, Memphis design patterns, synthwave motifs, and grid backgrounds. Best for: Retro collectors, synthwave vibes.

Ink Mystic

Minimalist

Black and white woodcut engraving style with high contrast, intricate crosshatching, Victorian occult art aesthetic. Best for: Minimalist editions, printing purposes.

Quick Tips for Best Results

  • Consistency: Generate all cards in one session with identical settings for a cohesive deck
  • Word Order: FLUX reads left-to-right. Put most important elements first in your prompt
  • Batch Generation: Generate Major Arcana first (most important), then one suit at a time
  • File Naming: Save as 01-the-fool-traditional.webp for easy organization
  • Post-Processing: Consider upscaling to 4K for print, adding card titles/numbers after generation
